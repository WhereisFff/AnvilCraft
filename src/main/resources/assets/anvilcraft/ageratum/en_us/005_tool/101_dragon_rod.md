## Dragon Rod

- The **Dragon Rod** simplifies the process of <gradient start="#ff00ff" end="#00e3ff"> "place Block Devourer → strike with Anvil Hammer → retrieve Block Devourer" </gradient> into a single tool
- All Dragon Rods function identically; only durability and attributes differ

## Usage

- Operating the Dragon Rod is very simple
  1. Left-click to break blocks within a certain range
  2. Right-click to toggle the range size: 3x3, 5x5, 7x7, and 9x9
  3. When holding the Dragon Rod and aiming at a block, the range outline is displayed
- The 3x3 range does not consume durability; subsequent sizes consume 1, 2, and 4 durability respectively
- When the Dragon Rod's durability is depleted, it does not break completely but loses all functionality, similar to **Elytra**

## When Breaking

The Dragon Rod follows Block Devourer rules: when mining world base blocks (**Stone**, **Netherrack**, **End Stone**), there is only a 5% chance of dropping. However, it cannot chain-gather top-side falling blocks
After mining once, the Dragon Rod has a cooldown period, defaulting to 1 second. This cooldown is only affected by the *Haste* and *Mining Fatigue* effects: each level of Haste reduces it by 4 ticks, and each level of Mining Fatigue increases it by 1 second
